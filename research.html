Research

Scientific questions around turbulence, chaos, and structure.

My work sits between fluid mechanics, nonlinear dynamics, machine learning, and geometric theory. The common aim is to make complicated systems more intelligible without simplifying away their real structure.

Core themes

Turbulence, dissipation, chaos, latent causality, geometric transport.

Typical tools

DNS, reduced-order models, variational arguments, explainable AI.

Style of work

Formal, scientific, and patient rather than spectacular.

Research areas

Main directions.

Wall turbulence · asymptotics

Geometric turbulence

Can dissipation, wall laws, and intermittency be derived from geometric, entropic, and fixed-point principles instead of being introduced only through empirical closure?

β-VAE · SURD · SHAP

Interpretable latent dynamics

Compression is useful only when latent coordinates relate back to coherent structures, causal organization, and the physical geometry of the flow.

Transformers · robustness

Prediction in chaotic systems

Model classes that stay robust in nonlinear dynamics and preserve long-term structure rather than optimizing a narrow predictive target.

Pressure · geometry · fields

Unified flow programs

Connecting pressure, information geometry, viscoelastic analogies, and galactic-scale formulations under a common mathematical vocabulary.

Methods

How the work gets done.

Fluid mechanics

Wall-bounded turbulence, shear flows, transport, coherent structures, and asymptotic arguments.

Machine learning

Representation learning, attention mechanisms, explainability tools, and reduced-complexity models.

Mathematics

Geometric interpretation, variational reasoning, nonlinear dynamics, and structure-first formulation.

Computation

DNS and numerical experiments, manuscript-driven coding, workflows that move between theory and data.

Current themes

Questions presently active.

  • Geometric turbulence

    Whether the Kolmogorov scale and the law of the wall can be tied to one deeper principle of path uncertainty and fixed-point structure.

  • Interpretable AI

    Latent causality with physical localization

    Whether causal relations inferred in latent space can be mapped back to wake structures, wall interactions, and flow organization reliably.

  • Nonlinear prediction

    Reduced-complexity attention for chaotic systems

    What simpler attention mechanisms can reveal about invariance, robustness, and temporal structure.
